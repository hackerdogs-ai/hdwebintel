# Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) — Audit & Compliance Pillar

## 1. Overview & Purpose

Audit & Compliance ensures **regulatory adherence, risk mitigation, and governance excellence** across the enterprise. This SOP protects against compliance violations, regulatory penalties, audit failures, and ensures adherence to industry standards, regulatory frameworks, and governance requirements.

**Key Threats Addressed:**
- Regulatory compliance violations (SOX, GDPR, HIPAA, PCI DSS, FedRAMP)
- Audit failures and regulatory penalties
- Governance and risk management gaps
- Internal control deficiencies and weaknesses
- Data privacy and protection violations
- Financial reporting inaccuracies and fraud
- Third-party risk and vendor compliance failures
- Policy and procedure violations
- Documentation and evidence gaps
- Regulatory change management failures

---

## 2. Scope

* All **regulatory compliance frameworks** across the enterprise
* All **audit activities and assessments** (internal and external)
* All **governance and risk management** processes
* All **policy and procedure management** systems
* All **compliance monitoring and reporting** activities

---

## 3. Roles & Responsibilities

### **Strategic Roles**

* **Chief Compliance Officer (CCO)** → defines compliance strategy, risk appetite, and governance
* **Principal Audit & Compliance Architect** → designs compliance architecture and control frameworks
* **Head of Audit & Compliance / Compliance Manager** → owns operational execution and regulatory adherence
* **Chief Risk Officer (CRO)** → ensures risk management integration and compliance alignment
* **Head of Risk & Compliance** → ensures adherence to regulatory requirements and industry standards

### **Execution Roles**

* **Compliance Analyst** → implements compliance controls, monitoring, and reporting
* **Internal Auditor** → conducts internal audits and compliance assessments
* **Risk Analyst** → manages risk assessments and compliance risk analysis
* **Policy Manager** → develops and maintains compliance policies and procedures
* **Regulatory Affairs Specialist** → manages regulatory relationships and compliance requirements
* **External Audit Coordinator** → coordinates external audits and regulatory examinations
* **Compliance Training Specialist** → develops and delivers compliance training programs
* **Incident Responder (Compliance)** → handles compliance violations and regulatory incidents

### **Appendix B: Compliance Incident Severity Levels**

**Critical (P1):**
- Regulatory compliance violation with penalty risk
- Audit failure with regulatory impact
- Compliance control failure with widespread impact
- Regulatory breach with legal consequences

**High (P2):**
- Multiple compliance violations with regulatory impact
- Compliance audit findings with business impact
- Compliance control gaps with regulatory exposure
- Compliance policy violations with regulatory impact

**Medium (P3):**
- Single compliance violation with limited impact
- Compliance control issues without regulatory impact
- Compliance policy gaps without regulatory exposure
- Compliance training gaps without regulatory impact

**Low (P4):**
- Compliance monitoring alerts and notifications
- Compliance configuration issues without regulatory impact
- Compliance training gaps and awareness
- Compliance documentation updates
