# Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) — Vendor Management Pillar

## 1. Overview & Purpose

Vendor Management ensures **secure, compliant, and risk-aware** third-party relationships across the enterprise. This SOP protects against vendor-related risks, supply chain attacks, and ensures adherence to vendor management best practices, due diligence, and regulatory requirements.

**Key Threats Addressed:**
- Vendor security breaches and data exposure
- Supply chain attacks and vendor compromise
- Vendor compliance violations and regulatory failures
- Vendor risk assessment gaps and evaluation failures
- Vendor access control failures and privilege abuse
- Vendor monitoring gaps and visibility issues
- Vendor incident response and breach notification failures
- Vendor contract and SLA violations
- Vendor data protection and privacy failures
- Vendor business continuity and disaster recovery gaps

---

## 2. Scope

* All **vendor relationships** across the enterprise
* All **vendor security** and risk management
* All **vendor compliance** and regulatory frameworks
* All **vendor analytics** and risk assessment
* All **vendor management testing and validation**

---

## 3. Roles & Responsibilities

### **Strategic Roles**

* **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** → defines vendor management strategy, risk appetite, and governance
* **Principal Vendor Management Architect** → designs comprehensive vendor management architecture and controls
* **Head of Vendor Management / Vendor Risk Manager** → owns operational execution and compliance
* **Chief Technology Officer (CTO)** → ensures vendor management integration in development processes
* **Head of Risk & Compliance** → ensures adherence to vendor management standards and regulations

### **Execution Roles**

* **Vendor Management Engineer** → implements vendor management controls, risk assessment, and monitoring
* **Vendor Risk Specialist** → manages vendor risk assessment and evaluation
* **Vendor Compliance Specialist** → manages vendor compliance and regulatory adherence
* **Vendor Security Specialist** → manages vendor security and access controls
* **Vendor Management Tester** → conducts vendor management testing and vulnerability assessments
* **Vendor Analytics Specialist** → manages vendor analytics and risk assessment
* **Vendor Incident Response Specialist** → manages vendor incident response and breach notification
* **Incident Responder (Vendor Management)** → handles vendor management breaches and incidents

### **Appendix B: Vendor Management Incident Severity Levels**

**Critical (P1):**
- Successful vendor management system compromise with data loss
- Vendor management system failure with vendor impact
- Vendor security breach with business impact
- Vendor management system outage with business impact

**High (P2):**
- Multiple vendor management vulnerabilities with security impact
- Vendor management control failures with vendor exposure
- Vendor management compliance violations with regulatory impact
- Vendor management system performance issues with vendor impact

**Medium (P3):**
- Single vendor management vulnerability with limited impact
- Vendor management security issues without vendor exposure
- Vendor management control issues without security impact
- Vendor management compliance gaps without regulatory impact

**Low (P4):**
- Vendor management monitoring alerts and notifications
- Vendor management configuration issues without security impact
- Vendor management training gaps and awareness
- Vendor management documentation updates
