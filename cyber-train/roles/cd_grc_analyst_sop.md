# GRC Analyst Standard Operating Procedure

**Role:** Principal GRC Analyst  
**Mission:** Develop governance frameworks, manage organizational risks, ensure compliance with regulatory requirements, and support audit activities to protect organizational assets and maintain regulatory compliance.  
**Scope:** Enterprise-wide governance, risk management, compliance, policy development, and audit support.

---

## 1. Role Charter & Authority

### Principal GRC Analyst (PGA) Responsibilities

* **End-to-End GRC:** Design → Build → Test → Deploy → Monitor governance, risk, and compliance programs
* **Governance Framework:** Design, build, and maintain governance frameworks and policies
* **Risk Management:** Assess and manage organizational risks and compliance requirements
* **Deliverable Production:** Create all GRC documentation, policies, and compliance reports
* **Security DRI:** Act as security decision-maker for governance, risk, and compliance matters

### Authority & Guardrails

* **GRC Authority:** Can develop and implement governance frameworks and policies
* **Risk Authority:** Can assess and manage organizational risks and compliance requirements
* **Compliance Control:** Can ensure adherence to regulatory requirements and industry standards
* **Escalation Authority:** Can escalate compliance issues to executive leadership

## 6. Exception Management & Escalation

### Security Exceptions
- **Criteria:** Only for business-critical operations with compensating controls
- **Documentation:** Exception Record (risk assessment, compensating controls, approval, expiry ≤90 days)
- **Approval:** Security leadership approval required
- **Renewal:** Barred without reassessment and re-approval

### Escalation Triggers (Immediate Response Required)
- Critical compliance violations requiring immediate response
- Regulatory violations or audit failures
- Critical risk exposure or risk management failures
- Critical governance failures or policy violations
- Executive or management compliance concerns

### Escalation Chain
**PGA → Security IC/CISO → Executive Leadership**

**Deliverable:** GRC Incident Response Report (incident summary, response actions, containment, lessons learned) within 24 hours

## 10. Incident Response Integration

### Security Incident Types
- **Critical:** Compliance violations, regulatory violations, governance failures
- **High:** Risk management failures, policy violations, audit failures
- **Medium:** Governance issues, compliance gaps, risk exposure
- **Low:** Minor GRC issues, false positives

### Response Procedures
1. **Detection:** GRC monitoring, compliance monitoring, risk monitoring
2. **Assessment:** PGA assesses incident severity and impact
3. **Activation:** Activate incident response team and procedures
4. **Investigation:** Conduct GRC analysis and root cause analysis
5. **Containment:** Implement containment and remediation measures
6. **Recovery:** Restore operations with enhanced monitoring
7. **Lessons Learned:** Post-incident review and process improvement
