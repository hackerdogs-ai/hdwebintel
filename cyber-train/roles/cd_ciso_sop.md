# Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) Standard Operating Procedure

**Role:** Chief Information Security Officer  
**Mission:** Establish and maintain enterprise cybersecurity strategy, governance, and risk management to protect organizational assets and ensure business continuity.  
**Scope:** Enterprise-wide cybersecurity program, risk management, compliance, governance, and executive security leadership.

---

## 1. Role Charter & Authority

### Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) Responsibilities

* **Strategic Leadership:** Develop and execute enterprise cybersecurity strategy aligned with business objectives
* **Risk Management:** Establish enterprise-wide risk management framework and governance
* **Compliance & Governance:** Ensure regulatory compliance and implement security governance structure
* **Executive Communication:** Provide board-level security briefings and executive reporting
* **Security Program Management:** Oversee comprehensive cybersecurity program and security operations

### Authority & Guardrails

* **Strategic Authority:** Can define enterprise security strategy and risk appetite
* **Budget Authority:** Can allocate security budget and resources
* **Policy Authority:** Can establish and enforce security policies and standards
* **Incident Authority:** Can activate crisis management and incident response
* **Compliance Authority:** Can mandate security compliance and training requirements

## 3. End-to-End Security Process

### Phase 1: Strategy & Governance (Strategic Planning)

| Step | Goal | Input to Step | Output Deliverable | Essential Content |
|------|------|----------------|-------------------|------------------|
| **1. Cybersecurity Strategy Development** | Define enterprise security strategy | Business objectives, risk assessment, threat landscape | **Cybersecurity Strategy Document** | Vision, mission, goals, objectives, roadmap, success metrics |
| **2. Risk Management Framework** | Establish enterprise risk management | Business context, threat intelligence, compliance requirements | **Risk Management Framework** | Risk appetite, tolerance, assessment methodology, treatment strategies |
| **3. Security Policy Development** | Create comprehensive security policies | Strategy, risk framework, compliance requirements | **Security Policy Suite** | Policies, standards, procedures, guidelines, enforcement mechanisms |
| **4. Governance Structure** | Implement security governance | Strategy, policies, organizational structure | **Security Governance Framework** | Roles, responsibilities, committees, decision-making processes |
| **5. Compliance Program** | Ensure regulatory compliance | Applicable regulations, business requirements | **Compliance Program** | Compliance framework, controls, monitoring, reporting |

### Phase 2: Risk Management & Assessment (Risk Operations)

| Step | Tool/Procedure | Risk Management Integration | Input to Step | Output Deliverable | Essential Content |

## 5. Success Metrics & KPIs

### Strategic & Governance Metrics
- **≥ 95%** board satisfaction with security briefings
- **≥ 90%** security strategy alignment with business objectives
- **≤ 5%** security budget variance from plan

### Risk Management Metrics
- **≤ 10%** critical risks without treatment plans
- **≥ 85%** risk assessment coverage of critical assets
- **≤ 30 days** average risk treatment implementation time

### Compliance Metrics
- **≥ 95%** compliance with applicable regulations
- **≤ 5%** audit findings without remediation plans
- **≥ 90%** security training completion rate

### Security Operations Metrics
- **≤ 4 hours** mean time to detect security incidents
- **≤ 24 hours** mean time to respond to security incidents
- **≥ 95%** security monitoring coverage of critical systems

### Executive Communication Metrics
- **≥ 90%** stakeholder satisfaction with security communication
- **≤ 48 hours** executive notification of critical security incidents
- **≥ 95%** security metrics accuracy and timeliness

## 6. Exception Management & Escalation

### Security Exceptions
- **Criteria:** Only for business-critical initiatives with compensating controls
- **Documentation:** Exception Record (risk assessment, compensating controls, approval, expiry ≤90 days)
- **Approval:** Board or executive approval required for critical exceptions
- **Renewal:** Barred without reassessment and re-approval

### Escalation Triggers (Immediate Response Required)
- Critical security incidents affecting business operations
- Regulatory compliance violations or audit failures
- Significant security budget overruns or resource constraints
- Major security policy violations or enforcement issues
- Critical vendor security incidents or failures
- Executive or board security concerns or questions

### Escalation Chain
**CISO → Board/Executive Leadership → Legal/External Resources**

**Deliverable:** Executive Security Report (incident summary, impact assessment, response actions, lessons learned) within 24 hours

- **Applicable Regulations** (SOX, GDPR, HIPAA, PCI DSS, SOC2)

- **Industry Standards** (ISO 27001, SOC2, PCI DSS)

**Deliverable:** Compliance Mapping Matrix maintained in security documentation with:
- Regulation → controls/requirements mapping
- Framework → implementation guidance

## 9. Security Governance Gates

### Mandatory Security Approvals
**Block initiatives unless ALL of the following pass:**

1. **Risk Assessment** completed with acceptable risk level
2. **Compliance Review** passed with no regulatory violations
3. **Security Architecture** approved with adequate controls
4. **Budget Approval** obtained with adequate resources
5. **Stakeholder Alignment** achieved with business objectives
6. **Executive Approval** obtained for critical initiatives

### Security Governance Process
1. **Risk Assessment:** Comprehensive risk assessment and treatment planning
2. **Compliance Review:** Regulatory compliance and audit requirements
3. **Security Review:** Security architecture and control requirements
4. **Budget Approval:** Resource allocation and financial planning
5. **Stakeholder Alignment:** Business unit coordination and approval
6. **Executive Approval:** Board or executive leadership approval

## 10. Incident Response Integration

### Security Incident Types
- **Critical:** Business-critical security incidents, regulatory violations
- **High:** Significant security incidents, compliance issues
- **Medium:** Security incidents with business impact
- **Low:** Minor security incidents, policy violations

### Response Procedures
1. **Detection:** Security monitoring, incident reporting, threat intelligence
2. **Assessment:** CISO assesses severity and business impact
3. **Activation:** Activate incident response team and crisis management
4. **Communication:** Notify stakeholders and executive leadership
5. **Response:** Coordinate response activities and containment
6. **Recovery:** Restore operations and implement improvements
7. **Lessons Learned:** Post-incident review and process improvement
