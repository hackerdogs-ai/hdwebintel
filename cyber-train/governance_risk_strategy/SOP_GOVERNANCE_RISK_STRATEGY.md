# Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) — Governance Risk Strategy Pillar

## 1. Overview & Purpose

Governance Risk Strategy ensures **strategic, comprehensive, and risk-aware** cybersecurity governance across the enterprise. This SOP protects against governance gaps, risk management failures, and ensures adherence to governance best practices, risk assessment, and regulatory requirements.

**Key Threats Addressed:**
- Governance gaps and control failures
- Risk management failures and assessment gaps
- Strategic security misalignment and investment failures
- Regulatory compliance violations and audit failures
- Risk appetite misalignment and tolerance violations
- Security governance maturity gaps and control weaknesses
- Risk assessment failures and security misconfigurations
- Governance policy violations and enforcement gaps
- Risk communication failures and stakeholder misalignment
- Strategic security planning and roadmap failures

---

## 2. Scope

* All **cybersecurity governance** across the enterprise
* All **risk management** and assessment systems
* All **strategic security planning** and roadmaps
* All **governance compliance** and regulatory frameworks
* All **governance security testing and validation**

---

## 3. Roles & Responsibilities

### **Strategic Roles**

* **Chief Information Security Officer (CISO)** → defines governance strategy, risk appetite, and governance
* **Principal Governance Architect** → designs comprehensive governance architecture and controls
* **Head of Governance Risk / Risk Management Manager** → owns operational execution and compliance
* **Chief Technology Officer (CTO)** → ensures governance integration in development processes
* **Head of Risk & Compliance** → ensures adherence to governance standards and regulations

### **Execution Roles**

* **Governance Risk Engineer** → implements governance controls, risk management, and monitoring
* **Risk Assessment Specialist** → manages risk assessments and evaluations
* **Governance Compliance Analyst** → manages governance compliance and regulatory adherence
* **Strategic Security Planner** → manages strategic security planning and roadmaps
* **Governance Security Tester** → conducts governance security testing and vulnerability assessments
* **Risk Communication Specialist** → manages risk communication and stakeholder engagement
* **Governance Policy Manager** → manages governance policies and procedures
* **Incident Responder (Governance Risk)** → handles governance failures and risk incidents

### **Appendix B: Governance Risk Security Incident Severity Levels**

**Critical (P1):**
- Successful governance risk system compromise with data loss
- Governance risk system failure with compliance impact
- Regulatory compliance violation with business impact
- Governance risk system outage with business impact

**High (P2):**
- Multiple governance risk vulnerabilities with security impact
- Governance risk control failures with compliance violations
- Governance risk compliance violations with regulatory impact
- Governance risk system performance issues with compliance impact

**Medium (P3):**
- Single governance risk vulnerability with limited impact
- Governance risk security issues without compliance impact
- Governance risk control issues without security impact
- Governance risk compliance gaps without regulatory impact

**Low (P4):**
- Governance risk security monitoring alerts and notifications
- Governance risk configuration issues without security impact
- Governance risk security training gaps and awareness
- Governance risk security documentation updates
