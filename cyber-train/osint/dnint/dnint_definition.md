## 1. Pillar Overview ðŸŒ

**Definition:**
Digital Network Intelligence (DNINT) focuses on the systematic collection, monitoring, and analysis of digital network traffic, infrastructure, and communications patterns to reveal hidden relationships, adversarial activities, vulnerabilities, and emerging threats. Unlike SIGINT (signals-level interception) or COMINT (content-level communications), DNINT emphasizes **network-layer intelligence**: domains, routing, IP addresses, DNS, BGP, TLS certificates, and traffic metadata.

**Purpose:**

* Map adversary digital infrastructure (C2 servers, proxy networks, malicious domains).
* Detect covert communications and traffic manipulation.
* Identify vulnerabilities, misconfigurations, or malicious routing activity.
* Support attribution through network telemetry correlation.
* Enable defensive actions such as takedowns, blackholing, and patch prioritization.

**Scope:**
DNINT spans open-source telemetry, passive/active network scanning, and digital forensics, while maintaining strict legal and compliance alignment. It directly supports **cybersecurity, counterintelligence, and law enforcement operations**.
